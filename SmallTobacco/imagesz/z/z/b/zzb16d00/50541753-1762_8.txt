‘Thus, it appears that insertion of a construct producing
high levels of tropomyosin-1 in normal celis results in pro-
duction of atypical cytoskeletal structures. Whether this has
consequences for cytoskelctal function and stability or affects
other aspects of cell behavior remains to be determined. It is,
however, a point which must be considered in any efforts to
control neoplastic growth through tropomyosin replacement.
Nevertheless, it is important to point out that homodimers of
tropomyosin-1 by themselves do participate in the reemer-
gence of cytoskeletal architecture in the DT/S cell lines and,
through a mechanism not understood presently, are responsi-
ble for the suppression of the neoplastic phenotype of DT
cells.
