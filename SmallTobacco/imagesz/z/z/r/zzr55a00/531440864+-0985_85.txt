To be sure, no one but the tobacco companies still seriously questions
the dangers of smoking. Wyden emphasizes that he and his colleagues want
tobacco products regulated, not banned.

One can admire that intent without admiring some of the methods,
Congressional investigative hearings can rarely be described as pretty, but
the tobacco flap has been especially ugly. Bitter attacks and
counterattacks -- the temptation of both sides to demonize -- can only add
to public doubts about the effectiveness of Congress,

When Waxman's subcommittee resumes hearings June 20 to go over the
contested documents, here's hoping both sides will be more amenable to
rational discourse.
